What Blockchain Is and How it Works

You’ve probably heard of bitcoin, but have you heard of blockchain? It’s the technology behind cryptocurrency -- and it’s changing the way information is stored and exchanged. Blockchain is a digital ledger that can record and track anything of value, like financial transactions, goods, property, medical records, or certification of a creative asset.

Rather than storing information in a central database, like we’re used to, blockchains store information across a network of computers. Information is collected in groups called blocks, which are then linked together to form a chain.

  • Increased Efficiency Blockchain is built on a peer-to-peer system, which eliminates the need for lawyers, banks, and other third parties. By streamlining and automating, transactions can be completed faster and more efficiently--which often means reduced cost.
  • Safety through Decentralization Since blockchain stores data on a widespread network of computers, data is safer from fraud and unauthorized activity.
  • Enhanced Security Each block of information in blockchain contains data about the blocks surrounding it, so no one can alter or remove a block without affecting the rest of the chain. This keeps data safe and secure.
  • Greater Transparency Data on a blockchain is more accurate, consistent, and available to all participants
    who have permissioned access. Privacy is ensured through the use of
    private keys and cryptography, while still maintaining transparency of the data.
  • Reliability with a Trustless System The combination of cryptography, verification by many computers, and the
    requirement for the whole network to agree ensures that we can trust every
    block on the chain. This creates what is called a “trustless” environment,
    which eliminates the need for intermediaries like banks or lawyers.
